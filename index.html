Issac Newton

was an English mathematician.

Here's a time line of Issac Newton life:

  • 1642 - Born in Lincolnshire, England
  • 1933 - Isaac Newton was born (according to the Julian calendar, in use in England at the time) on Christmas Day, 25 December 1642 (NS 4 January 1643[a]) "an hour or two after midnight",[7] at Woolsthorpe Manor in Woolsthorpe-by-Colsterworth, a hamlet in the county of Lincolnshire. His father, also named Isaac Newton, had died three months before.
  • 1659 - He was removed from school and returned to Woolsthorpe-by-Colsterworth by October 1659. His mother, widowed for the second time, attempted to make him a farmer, an occupation he hated.[12] Henry Stokes, master at The King's School, persuaded his mother to send him back to school.
  • 1661 - he was admitted to Trinity College, Cambridge, on the recommendation of his uncle Rev William Ayscough, who had studied there. He started as a subsizar—paying his way by performing valet's duties—until he was awarded a scholarship in 1664, guaranteeing him four more years until he could get his MA.
  • 1667 - he returned to Cambridge and in October was elected as a fellow of Trinity.
